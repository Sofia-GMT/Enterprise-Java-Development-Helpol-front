What is a primer?

In simplified form, primers are DNA molecules formed by the concatenation of units called nucleotides. According to their chemical nature, there are four main types of nucleotides, represented by the letters A, T, C and G. From a computational point of view, we simplifly the concept of primer by summarizing it as a sequence of those letters.

Why they are useful?

In molecular biology, a class of proteins called polymerases are frequently used. These are responsible for extending nucleotide chains, and at the beginning of this elongation they need to use primers.
